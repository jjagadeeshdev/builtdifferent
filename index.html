The Ford Mustang is an American automobile manufactured and marketed by Ford since 1964, as Ford's longest nameplate in continuous production. Currently in its seventh generation, it is the fifth-best selling Ford car nameplate. The namesake of the "pony car" automobile segment, the Mustang was developed as a highly styled line of sporty coupes and convertibles derived from existing model lines, initially distinguished by its pronounced "long hood, short deck" proportions.

The Dodge Challenger is an iconic American muscle car, with the 2023-2024 models marking the end of the third generation, featuring 5-passenger seating, retro styling, and a lineup ranging from efficient V6s to high-output V8s. The Chevrolet Corvette is an iconic American two-seater sports car produced since 1953, currently in its 8th generation (C8). The C8, featuring a revolutionary mid-engine layout, offers superior performance with a 6.2L V8 (Stingray) or a high-revving 5.5L V8 (Z06). Key highlights include advanced, driver-focused, tech-forward interiors, a 0-60 mph time of 2.6 seconds (Z06), and a 2026 ZR1 with 1,064 horsepower.

The Pontiac Firebird is an American automobile built and produced by Pontiac from the 1967 to 2002 model years. Designed as a pony car to compete with the Ford Mustang, it was introduced on February 23, 1967, five months after GM's Chevrolet division's platform-sharing Camaro.

It is the successor of the A80 Supra, which ceased production in 2002.
